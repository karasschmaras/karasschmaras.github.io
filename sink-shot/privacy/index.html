CupClash Privacy Policy

Effective date: 19 July 2026

The short version: CupClash works without an account. You never give us your name, email, or phone number. We store your matches and a display name you choose, on servers in the EU. Ads are non-personalized only — we do not track you, build ad profiles, or share data for advertising. You can delete everything from inside the app.

2. What data the game processes

Anonymous account

When you first start CupClash, Firebase Authentication creates an anonymous account: a random user ID (uid). That is all it is — no email, no name, no phone number, no password. We cannot identify who you are from it. The uid exists so your matches, progress, and purchases can belong to something without you having to sign up.

Display name

You choose a display name inside the app. It is shown to the people you play against. Pick whatever you like — it does not have to be (and does not need to be) your real name.

Match data

Playing online (matchmaking or invite links) stores match documents on our servers: the two players' uids, the game state, a record of each turn (the throws and their results), and timestamps. Your game progress — stats, Splash balance, and unlocked cosmetics — is stored with your anonymous profile. While you wait for matchmaking, a short-lived queue ticket exists and is deleted as soon as you are paired. Invite links store the invite code and which match it belongs to.

Push notifications (opt-in)

If you allow notifications when the operating system asks (and only then), the game stores a Firebase Cloud Messaging push token for your device so we can send “Your turn” and “Match over” notifications. Turning notifications off in the app or in your system settings stops the pushes; you can also delete the tokens by deleting your account (section 6).

Advertising (non-personalized only)

CupClash shows ads through Google AdMob, and only non-personalized ones (every ad request is flagged npa=1). That means:

  • No ad profiles are built about you, and no data is used to target you across apps.
  • The app never shows Apple’s App Tracking Transparency prompt — because it never tracks.
  • Where the law requires it (for example in the EU), a Google UMP consent dialog is shown before the first ad request. Even with consent, ads stay non-personalized.

To serve and measure ads and to prevent fraud, Google processes technical data such as your IP address and device information. See Google’s Privacy Policy and how Google uses data in AdMob. Buying the “Remove Ads” purchase removes interstitial ads entirely.

Purchases

In-app purchases are handled entirely by the Apple App Store or Google Play. We never see your payment details. The game only stores which non-consumable products your account owns (for example the Remove-Ads entitlement) so they stay unlocked.

What we do not collect

  • No email address, real name, phone number, contacts, photos, or location.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs beyond the ad serving described above.
  • No chat — the only text other players ever see from you is your display name.

3. Why we may process it (legal bases)

  • To run the game you asked for (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): anonymous account, match data, display name, purchases, opt-in push notifications.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): keeping the service secure, fair, and free of abuse (for example server-side game rules and fraud prevention).
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): the ad consent collected via the UMP dialog where required. You can withdraw it any time in Settings → consent management.

4. Where your data lives

Game data is stored with Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore, Cloud Functions) in the EU region europe-west3 (Frankfurt, Germany). Google acts as our processor. Where any Google service involves transfers outside the EU/EEA, they are covered by the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and Google’s participation in the EU–US Data Privacy Framework.

5. How long we keep it

  • Account and profile data (uid, display name, progress, push tokens): kept while your account exists.
  • Matchmaking queue tickets: deleted the moment you are paired or leave the queue.
  • Matches: matches idle for 7 days are closed automatically. Match and turn records are kept as the match history of both participants.

6. Deleting your data

Use Settings → account & data deletion in the app. This deletes your anonymous account, display name, progress, and push tokens from our servers. Finished match records may be retained because they equally belong to your opponent’s match history — but after deletion the uid in them no longer refers to any existing account, and it never referred to an identified person in the first place. Store purchases remain restorable through Apple or Google.

You can also email karasleon@web.de for any deletion request. Note that because accounts are anonymous, we may need information from your device (your uid, shown in Settings) to find your data at all.

7. Your rights (GDPR)

You have the right to access, rectify, and erase your data, to restrict or object to processing, to data portability, and to withdraw any consent at any time (without affecting past processing). You also have the right to complain to a data protection supervisory authority — in Germany, the authority of your federal state.

To exercise any right, use the in-app options above or email karasleon@web.de.

8. Children

CupClash is a general-audience game with an alcohol-free theme. It is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children. Because the game requires no personal details, shows only non-personalized ads, and does no tracking or behavioral advertising for anyone, the same protections apply to every player regardless of age.

9. Changes to this policy

If the game’s data flows change (for example new features or services), we will update this policy, change the effective date at the top, and — for significant changes — point it out in the app. The current version is always at this address.

Privacy Policy — Brainrot Farm

Last updated: 17 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information Brainrot Farm ("the App," "the Game," "we," "us," "our") collects, why, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have. It is written in plain language. By using the App you agree to the practices described here.

1. Who we are (data controller) and how to contact us

  • Studio / data controller: Karas Games (sole proprietor Leon Karas), Germany.
  • App: Brainrot Farm, a single-player idle/collection game for iOS and Android.
  • Privacy contact: Leon Karas — karasleon@web.de

For any privacy question, or to exercise a right described in Section 7, email karasleon@web.de with the subject line "Privacy Request — Brainrot Farm."

2. The short version

  • The App is free to play, single-player, and has no account, no login, and no first-party server. Your game progress is saved on your device.
  • We show opt-in rewarded ads through Google AdMob. AdMob is the only third-party service in the App — we use no separate analytics provider and run no server of our own.
  • AdMob collects some data — chiefly an advertising identifier, ad and in-app interactions, and approximate (region-level) location — to show ads and to measure and protect them.
  • On Apple devices, ad tracking only happens if you allow it (the App Tracking Transparency prompt). In the EEA/UK, we ask for your consent first (the Google consent form). If you say no, the game still works and you still get every reward — you just see non-personalized ads.
  • We do not collect your name, email, contacts, photos, precise location, or payment-card details. Purchases are handled entirely by Apple / Google, not by us.
  • The App is rated 4+ / Everyone but is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

The detailed version follows.

4. What we DO NOT collect

To be unambiguous, the App does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or postal address.
  • Any account or login identifier — there is no account and no login.
  • Payment or card details — all purchases are processed by Apple (StoreKit) or Google (Play Billing); we never see or store your financial information.
  • Your precise GPS location, contacts, photos/camera, microphone audio, health data, messages, or browsing history.
  • User-generated content — the collectible "reveal cards" you can share are created on your device and shared through your phone's own share sheet; the image is not sent to us or collected by us.
  • First-party analytics — we do not run our own analytics SDK or server. The only data collected is by Google AdMob, described in Section 3.

7. Your rights and choices

7.1 Everyone — consent and ad choices

  • App Tracking Transparency (Apple): on iOS, the App asks before any cross-app ad tracking. You can change this anytime in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.
  • Consent (EEA/UK): we show a Google consent form before personalized ads. You can withdraw or change your consent at any time from the in-app Privacy / Consent settings (which re-opens the consent form).
  • Reset/limit your advertising ID: iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking / Apple Advertising; Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.
  • Declining costs you nothing in-game: if you decline tracking or consent, you still get non-personalized ads and every reward in full.

7.2 GDPR / UK-GDPR (EEA, UK, and similar laws)

You have the right to: access the personal data held about you; request correction; request erasure ("right to be forgotten"); restrict or object to processing; data portability; and to withdraw consent at any time. Because we hold no first-party server-side record tied to you, most data is held by our SDK partner — we will help route your request to them, and you can also exercise rights directly with Google via its privacy controls. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your data protection authority (in Germany, the relevant Landesdatenschutzbehörde).

To exercise a right, email karasleon@web.de.

7.3 California / US state privacy (CCPA/CPRA and similar)

California residents (and residents of similar US states) have the right to know what categories of personal information are collected, the right to delete it, the right to correct it, and the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information and of targeted advertising.

"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information." We do not sell your personal information for money. However, our use of advertising identifiers with Google AdMob for personalized advertising may be considered a "sale" or "sharing" under some US state laws. You can opt out of this by declining the consent/tracking prompt (Apple ATT / the Google consent form) or by emailing karasleon@web.de with "Do Not Sell or Share" in the subject. Honoring your tracking/consent choice opts you out of this sharing for advertising. We do not knowingly sell or share the personal information of anyone under 16.

7.4 How to make a request

Email karasleon@web.de. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law. We may need to confirm your request relates to your device/install (we have no account to verify against), and we will not discriminate against you for exercising a right.

8. Data retention

  • On-device game save: kept on your device until you delete it. Deleting the App removes your local save. We hold no copy.
  • Third-party SDK data (AdMob): retained by Google per its retention policies (see Section 5). We do not control its retention periods; review Google's privacy policy for specifics.
  • We keep no first-party database of player personal data, so there is no server-side record for us to retain or delete on our own systems.

9. Children's privacy (COPPA and age-appropriate design)

  • Brainrot Farm is rated 4+ / Everyone, but it is a general-audience game that is NOT directed to children. Its content, theme, and presentation are intended for a broad, general audience.
  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under the age defined as a "child" in your jurisdiction). The App has no account, no login, and no profile.
  • The App is not part of Apple's "Made for Kids" / "Kids" category or Google's "Designed for Families" program, because it shows third-party advertising (which those programs restrict). The age category is set to "Not applicable," never "Made for Kids."
  • Where required by law (e.g. Texas SB 2420), in-app purchases are gated on the age signal provided by the app store: if the store indicates a minor account without verifiable parental consent, the App will not complete a purchase and defers to the store's parental-consent process.
  • Parents/guardians: if you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the App, contact us at karasleon@web.de and we will take steps to delete it and to route a deletion request to our SDK partner. You may also use your device's parental controls / Screen Time / Family Link and your app store's purchase-approval settings to manage your child's use and spending.

10. Purchases and subscriptions

The App offers optional in-app purchases (Diamantini currency packs) and an auto-renewable subscription (Auto-Collect / "Auto-Raccolta," available in 30-day, 90-day, and 1-year terms). All payments are handled by Apple or Google — we do not receive or store your payment details, and we do not collect any purchase or transaction data ourselves (we run no analytics or backend). Purchase terms, refunds, and subscription management are governed by your app store and by our Terms of Service and EULA.

11. Security

We minimize what is collected and rely on the encryption and security practices of our platform and SDK partner (data in transit is encrypted via HTTPS). Because there is no first-party account or server holding your data, the attack surface for your personal data is small. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we design the App to collect as little as possible.

Material changes will be reflected in the App or its store listing. Your continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy. In particular, if we later add an analytics or attribution SDK, we will update this policy and the store privacy labels before that SDK collects any data.
